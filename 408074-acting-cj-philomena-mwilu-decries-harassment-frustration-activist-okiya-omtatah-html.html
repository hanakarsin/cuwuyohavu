Acting CJ Philomena Mwilu Decries Harassment, Frustration from Activist Okiya Omtatah

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Wednesday, February 14, 2024

- Acting CJ Philomena Mwilu said Okiya Omtatah was harassing her through a smear guerilla attack and campaign trying to wear her down into quitting

- Mwilu now wants Omtata to be restrained from instituting any claims or actions against her

- In December 2020, Omtatah moved to court to block Mwilu from taking over as the next president of the Supreme Court

Acting Chief Justice Philomena Mwilu has decried continuous harassment and frustrations from Activist Okiya Omtatah, hindering her from performing her duties effectively.

In her reply to an affidavit on Wednesday, April 7, the acting CJ claimed Omtatah was harassing her through a smear guerilla attack and campaign to wear her down into quitting and distracting her from performing her duties.

Mwilu wants Omtatah barred or restrained from instituting any claims or actions against her without the court's prior leave.

“The petitioner is a vexatious, frivolous and galloping litigant who is abusing the court process to achieve ulterior motives not connected with the vindication of the law,” read court papers filed by Mwilu.

In December 2020, Omtatah moved to court to block Mwilu from taking over as the Acting CJ, arguing she was not fit to hold the office upon David Maraga's retirement.

The activist wanted the court to bar the Judicial Service Commission from appointing Mwilu to act in the CJ office before clearing her of corruption allegations she was facing.

“Pending the inter-parties hearing and determination of this case, the court is pleased to issue a temporary order prohibiting JSC from appointing DCJ Mwilu to act in the office of the Chief Justice unless and until the JSC clears her of the allegations of corruption and abuse of office contained in the pending four petitions seeking her removal,” the activist told court.

Mwilu's woes began in 2018 when she was arrested at the Supreme Court's premises in Nairobi for alleged involvement in corrupt dealings involving the collapsed Imperial Bank.

She was subsequently released on a KSh 5 million personal bond and ordered to appear in court on August 29, 2018, to take a plea.
