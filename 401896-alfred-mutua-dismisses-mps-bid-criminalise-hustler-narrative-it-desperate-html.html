Maendeleo Chap Chap party leader Alfred Mutua has waded into the ongoing hustler versus dynasty debate that is being propagated by Deputy President William Ruto.

The Machakos governor criticised the plot by some Members of Parliament to criminalise the hustler narrative terming it a desperate move.

In a video shared on his Twitter page on Thursday, February 4, Mutua argued the narrative did not amount to hate speech and as such it should be fought politically.

"The issue of trying to criminalise the words dynasty and hustler is desperate. It's a political thing, fight it politically. I think the problem is the interpretation I understand how Swahili works, doctor in Swahili is daktari, hospital is hospitali and someone told me hustler is hasara," he said as he appeared to diss the word.

MPs want hustler narrative banned

The National Assembly's national security committee which is led by Kiambaa MP Paul Koinange had revealed plan to kill Deputy President William Ruto's hustler movement by criminalising his narrative which is gaining popularity.

The team spent a two-day retreat in Mombasa where they reviewed bills that they want tabled in order to amend the National Cohesion and Integration act (NCIC).

The lawmakers want class added as one of the basis of hate speech as the law only talks about discrimination on the basis of race, ethnicity, nationality and religion.

Section of leaders urge Ruto to drop slogan

President Uhuru Kenyatta and Opposition leader Raila Odinga are among leaders who have openly called on the DP to drop the potentially dangerous campaign strategy that they have noted could drag the country into murky waters.

Nyeri Two MP Ngunjiri Wambugu also claimed the ‘hustlers’ vs ‘dynasty’ narrative was dangerous at a fundamental level because it was convincing people that the reason they were not doing well was because of others.

“We will sadly learn that some hustlers are more hustlers than others. A hustler with a bicycle will view a hustler with a motorcycle as dynasty. Hustlers with one old wheelbarrow will see hustlers with three newer wheelbarrows as dynasties,” he recently stated.

Ruto, however, insists his so called hustler movement is not an enemy of anyone but that of poverty and unemployment.
