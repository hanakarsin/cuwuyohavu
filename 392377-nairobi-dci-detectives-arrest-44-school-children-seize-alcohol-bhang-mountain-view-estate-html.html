Nairobi: DCI detectives arrest 44 school children, seize alcohol and bhang in Mountain View Estate

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Friday, August 16, 2024

- Investigations are ongoing to establish how the youngsters sneaked out of their homes in Nairobi without raising suspicion

- The host, Millicent Kithinji was also arrested and was being questioned at the DCI offices in Dagoretti

- Cases of school-going children sneaking out of their homes to attend parties have been rife since schools closed in March

Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) detectives from Nairobi arrested 44 teenagers at Nairobi’s Mountain View Estate on the evening of Saturday, November 21.

The children, aged between 14 and 17 years old, included 26 boys and 18 girls. They were primary and high school learners.

The detectives also arrested their host, Millicent Kithinji, 41, who was questioned at Dagoretti. Officers. The DCI officers also seized different brands of alcohol and bhang during the arrests.

Preliminary investigations revealed the children had travelled from Nairobi, Kiambu and Machakos counties.

"A probe is ongoing to establish how the children sneaked from home and travelled far without raising suspicion and why Kithinji had hosted them," the DCI said, adding that parents of the learners had been contacted.

DCI warned severe action will be taken against grown-ups who abuse children.

The Saturday's arrest came hardly a day after detectives rescued about six children from Nairobi's Komarok area who disappeared about one week ago.

The children were found in Thika, Kiambu county, where they were lured by men who bought them gifts, alcohol and entertained them.

According to preliminary reports, the children were contacted by an international number and before they left, they claimed they were going to attend a music audition.

The suspects are still at large and are being pursued by the police.

The cases bring to the fore the magnitude of danger school children are exposed to as they continue to stay at home.

Learners have been out of school since mid-March 2020 when the government closed all schools following the outbreak of the COVID-19 pandemic in the country.

Grade Four, Class Eight and Form Four students returned in class on October 12.

Last week, Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha announced schools will fully reopen on January 4.
