IEBC confirms receiving Jubilee Party's 'fake' list of aspirants for Kibra seat which has Mariga's n

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Tuesday, April 30, 2024

- IEBC confirmed the letter was delivered at its registry on Monday, August 26

- The electoral body did not, however, divulge identity of the messenger

- Jubilee initially dismissed the letter terming it fake and asked members to wait for official communication

- The Kibra constituency by-election is scheduled for November 7, 2019

Confusion continues to rock Jubilee party leadership after the Independent Boundaries and Electoral Commission (IEBC) confirmed receipt of a list of its aspirants for the Kibra by-election.

In a statement, the IEBC said the mysterious letter was physically delivered at its central registry but the commission did not divulge identity of the messenger.

"IEBC received a letter, on Monday, August 26 2019 at 5pm, forwarding names of aspirants of Jubilee Party for the Kibra by election. However, it has come to the attention of the commission that Jubilee Party has denied authoring the said letter and termed it as fake," the commission said.

The ruling party had on Monday, August 26, dismissed the letter as fake via its social media handles and reminded members communication the nomination would be communicated later.

The letter was apparently signed by Secretary General Raphael Tuju and indicated five aspirants among them Kenyan soccer star McDonald Mariga would be battling for party ticket.

Should it turn out to be fake, the electoral body warned investigations would be conducted to pin down the culprit who may have authored it.

"We receive letters from all over the country at our registry delivered by post or physically delivered either in person or through messengers, if the letters is confirmed as fake, then the matter will be handled by investigative authorities," IEBC cautioned.

Mariga has neither showed any political interest and often chose to keep off politicians save for his visit to Deputy President William Ruto.

The Kibra constituency by-election is scheduled for November 7,2019.

The seat fell vacant after the death of Ken Okoth on July 26, 2019 after battling with cancer.
