Busia, West Pokot county assemblies vote to pass BBI bill

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Saturday, April 13, 2024

- Busia county assembly tabled the Building Bridges Initiative bill on Thursday, February 4

- The MCAs debated the proposed Constitutional Amendment Bill 2020 which saw only one ward representative oppose it

- In West Pokot assembly, all members unanimously voted to pass the bill to join Homa Bay, Siaya and Kisumu counties

The Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) proponents have at least 19 county assemblies to convince after Busia and West Pokot MCAs adopted the bill to become the fourth and the fifth respectively.

The two county assemblies on Tuesday, February 16, voted overwhelmingly in support of the President Uhuru Kenyatta-backed Constitutional Amendment Bill 2020.

Busia and West Pokot joined Siaya, Kisumu and Homa Bay counties which have already passed the bill.

24 county assemblies

At least 24 county assemblies will be required to endorse the document for it to move to the next stage when it will be officially introduced in the Senate and the National Assembly.

But the same will be submitted to the people in a referendum if either House of Parliament fails to pass the bill, or if the bill relates to a matter specified in Article 255 (1) of the Constitution.

Under Article 255(1), some areas of the Constitution must be subjected to a referendum if they are to be changed.

Baringo says NO!

Only Baringo county assembly has so far rejected the proposed amendments.

The MCAs debated the motion on Thursday, February 11, after which it was subjected to voting that saw only five members support it.
