Widowed Kenyan Celebrities Singlehandedly Raising Their Kids

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Wednesday, May 15, 2024

- Death is inevitable, and when it strikes, it leaves behind a void that cannot be filled

- These celebrities, unfortunately, lost their partners and are raising their kids as single parents

- They deserve to be celebrated and encouraged for the work they are doing

Often when people get into marriage, they envision spending the rest of their lives together. However, a happily ever after is not a guarantee.

In Kenya, we have celebrities who have lost their loved ones, leaving them to care for their children. While raising a child alongside a spouse is hard, singlehandedly raising kids is much more difficult.

Today we look at some of the celebrities who took up the responsibility of bringing up their kids following the death of their spouse.

Ruth Matete

The gospel musician and minister became famous after winning the Tusker Project Fame, which she later regretted.

Matete said the competition did not align with her values as a Christian but pocketed a cool KSh 5 million for her exploits on the stage.

Matete later met John Apewajoye, a Nigerian preacher, and they fell in love before exchanging vows in 2019.

The couple was expecting their daughter when John sustained third-degree burns following an accident at their Athi River home in 2020.

Sadly, he passed away in hospital.

Matete carried the pregnancy to term and delivered a beautiful baby girl she is raising with the help of her father, Abel Amunga.

Mohammed Noorani

Mohammed Noorani is the widower of the late news anchor Anjlee Gadhvi who lost the battle to cancer in January 2020.

Anjlee was diagnosed with liver cancer in 2013, and in subsequent years, cancer went into remission.

Nooran offered to donate part of his liver to Anjlee during her battle with the disease, but he was not a match.

They have two children together, and he took sole responsibility for them after her passing.

Ted Josiah

The legendary music producer turned leather bags designer is a doting dad to his young girl.

Ted Josiah was married to Reginah Katar, who died in 2017, three months after their daughter’s birth. Ted has never remarried and is singlehandedly raising their daughter.

He often shares heart-warming moments with his daughter on social media, shedding light on what single dads go through in raising children.

He even refers to himself as the man mom.

George Ikua

George Ikua is the husband of former NTV presenter Janet Kanini Ikua who passed away from lung cancer in 2017.
