Candidate for IEBC Job Makes 8 Grave Spelling Mistakes in 3-Paragraph Application Letter

A candidate seeking to become a commissioner for the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) was put on the spot over grave spelling mistakes in his application letter.

The interview panel led by Chairperson Elizabeth Muli on Thursday, July 23, said Kenya Water Towers Agency Board Member Timothy Ole Naeku had eight spelling mistakes of simple words.

According to the panel members, Naeku had wrongly spelt the words equipped, maintaining, exclusion, privilege, safeguard, commissioner, among others.

"In these three paragraphs, you have made very grave spelling mistakes. Eight spelling mistakes.... why would you say that happened?" posed the interviewer.

The panel questioned the member why there were such mistakes in his application, yet lawyers are expected to be keen with their work and proofread any document that goes out.

Timothy Naeku defends himself

Defending himself, the lawyer argued the mistakes were an oversight that could have been presented during the last process of the application.

"It must have been presented on the very last day of the closing of the application date," he said.

Naeku further told the panel his experience in handling election disputes would add value to the commission.

The seven-member panellist is seeking to replace four IEBC commissioners who resigned from the commission four years ago.

There are 36 candidates who had been shortlisted for the three vacant positions in the commission.

Last week former National Police Service Commissioner (NPSC) Abdalla Mohamed withdrew from the race over a forged degree certificate.

The oral interviews come to a close on Friday, July 2021.
