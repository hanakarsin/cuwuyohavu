Newspapers Review for March 17: Uhuru Urges NASA Principals to Stop Fighting, Unite Aginst 'Common E

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Wednesday, March 20, 2024

The papers on Wednesday, March 17, widely reported on the State House meeting that President Uhuru Kenyatta held with various political leaders on Tuesday, March 16

The dailies also reported about the ongoing investigations into the murder of veteran journalist Jennifer Itumbi Wambua.

1. Daily Nation

According to this publication, President Uhuru Kenyatta has asked National Super Alliance (NASA) co-principals to stop confusing Kenyans with their rivalry and instead forge a united front against the "common enemy".

The president met political big wigs led by Raila Odinga (ODM), Musalia Mudavadi (ANC), Charity Ngilu (Narc), Ford Kenya's Moses Wetang'ula, and Wiper's Kalonzo Musyoka at the State House, Nairobi, on Tuesday, March 16.

lthough a statement from State House indicated the leaders only discussed the alarming COVID-19 situation in Kenya, it has emerged politics also dominated the meeting.

Uhuru asked the leaders, particularly those from the National Super Alliance (NASA) to bury the hatchet for the sake of the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) and the coming referendum.

The leaders also castigated Deputy President William Ruto for polarising the country using his hustler movement and the United Democratic Alliance (UDA).

2. The Standard

The government has assured Kenyans that it is ready for the national primary and secondary schools national exams, The Standard reports.

Government Spokesperson Colonel Cyrus Oguna said the government had prepared well enough for the examinations to take place seamlessly.

Oguna said learners in schools that face disasters such as flooding or insecurity will also sit for their papers with no challenges.

At least 1.9 million primary and secondary school candidates are set to write their exams from Monday, March 22.

3. Taifa Leo

Taifa Leo reports on the speculations surrounding Tanzanian president John Pombe Magufuli's disappearance from the public eye, 18 days ago.

Analysts opined the Tanzania government's silence on this matter only pointed to a possibility of the health rumours being true.

Magufuli was last seen in public on February 27, and despite him being a staunch Christian who attends Sunday church service regularly, he has not been to any chapel for the past three Sundays.

4. The Star

According to this daily, the slain National Lands Commission (NLC) deputy director of communications Jennifer Itumbi Wambua lived in fear of her life in the days leading to her death.

Wambua was found dead on Monday, March 15, in Ngong Forest. She was a key prosecution witness in a KSh 122 million graft case at the Government Advertising Agency.

Some journalists who were in court when she testified in the case revealed she pleaded with them several times not to publish her testimonies.

5. People Daily

According to People Daily, the People's Empowerment Party (PEP) political party associated with Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria appears to be a proxy for Deputy President William Ruto in the Juja by-election.

This came after PEP fronted George Koimburi for the mini poll. Koimburi is considered to be one of the serious contenders in the race after he ditched Jubilee for Kuria's party.

However, the Tanga Tanga camp dismissed claims Ruto had a hand in choosing a flagbearer in the by-election and insisted he is only advancing the agenda of his party.

This came a couple of days after the DP's United Democratic Alliance (UDA) pulled out of the race in a statement posted on its social media platforms on Monday, March 15.
