Nosey man feels ex-Kiambu Governor William Kabogos wrath over grabbed land slur

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Wednesday, February 14, 2024

Former Kiambu governor William Kabogo became the latest public figure to show his unruly side after he went ham on a Twitter bully on Sunday, March 24.

With Kenyans growing increasingly agitated with the current state of the country from corruption scandals to starving citizens in the north, netizens have developed a queer mechanism to cope with all the stress – through throwing jibes online.

And it was Kabogo who was the latest celebrity to find himself hitting out at one Twitter user who attempted to poke some fun at him using one of the country’s most notorious vices in the form of land grabbing.

In a Twitter exchange seen by TUKO.co.ke, Kabogo brutally tackles one Abdirizakwarsam who decided to reign on an innocent photo post by the former Kiambu boss.

While Kabogo shared images of himself taking care of his lush garden, the Twitter bully offensively suggested the former governor was enjoying grabbed land.

Now the thing with political figures is they do not tolerate any form of trolling especially if it involves matters as sensitive as corruption or land grabbing.

For this reason, Kabogo did not hold back in his response and it was as brutal as they come:

“Your family land isn’t it? Last night I was with your mum and she didn’t say anything,” he rudely retorted much to the amusement of the online audience.

His response was almost similar to that of ODM party leader Raila Odinga’s last born daughter Winnie who, as TUKO.co.ke reported, also fended away an online bully in spectacular fashion as well.

Interestingly, Winnie was also irked by her bully who seemed to drag Raila’s name in the disheartening food shortage situation in the north which has been linked to deaths of several people in Turkana and Baringo counties.

Kabogo and Winnie Odinga’s responses towards Kenyans may have been savage, but that does not mean the trolling is set to stop anytime soon as Kenyans , based on the trials and tribulations they have been subjected to, will need an outlet.
