How to clear stretch marks naturally

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Tuesday, July 2, 2024

Stretch marks are something a lot of people have to deal with. Some do not give them a second thought, while others are seriously bothered by them. If you belong to the second category, then we recommend you to check out this page, where we are going to be talking about stretch marks, how to remove them, and other things you might want to know about this topic.

Stretch marks home remedies

There are lots of ways you can deal with stretch marks by using simple natural ingredients that are easily accessible for everyone. We have prepared lots of recipes you can try at home to make your stretch marks much less noticeable. So strap in and let’s go!

Shea butter on stretch marks

Shea butter is full of Vitamin A and antioxidants, which are great for improving the elasticity of the skin. If you want to clear your stretch marks, then shea butter should become your best friend! Use only fresh raw shea butter, as it is the most nutritious in its pure state. Here is what you should do with it:

    Coconut oil on stretch marks

    Another great weapon against those resilient stretch marks is coconut oil. It is frequently used as a beauty product, so it is no wonder that you can use it to deal with those annoying stretch marks. There are plenty of ways you can turn it into a stretch mark remedy, and here are some of them:

    • Get a scoop of coconut oil and gently rub it into the areas that are prone to having stretch marks. Leave the oil on until the skin absorbs it completely. Repeat this uncomplicated procedure every day.
    • Mix several drops of coconut oil with the same amount of olive or castor oil. Gently rub this mixture into the affected skin and leave it to absorb. Repeat every day.
    • Mix half a cup of liquefied coconut oil and an equal amount of coffee grounds. You can use it right away or pour this mixture into a mould, leave it for five hours and then store it in the fridge. Either way, gently rub this remedy on your skin for several minutes and thoroughly wash it off. Repeat the same process three times a week.

    Aloe vera on stretch marks

    Aloe vera is frequently used to heal various skin ailments, as it is full of nutrients. It can also help you in your fight against stretch marks. If they are just starting to show, aloe vera can be a great help in getting rid of them completely! Here are some of the many ways you can use it:

    • Get the thick gel from the aloe plant and apply it to the skin that is covered in stretch marks. Gently rub the gel it into the skin and leave it on for fifteen minutes. Use lukewarm water to rinse it off and repeat this process every day. Soon, you will notice that your stretch marks are fading away much faster.
    • Mix the aloe vera gel and coffee grounds to create a paste. Apply this sort of scrub to your stretch marks with massaging movements. Leave the paste on the skin for twenty minutes and then wipe it away with a warm wet towel. After you have cleaned the area, moisturise it with a lotion or olive oil to reinforce the effects of using aloe vera. Repeat the procedure every day.
    • You can make a moisturiser out of aloe vera. Get a tablespoon of aloe gel, a tablespoon of olive oil and a tablespoon of wheat oil. Mix them together, put the mixture in an old moisturiser bottle or an airtight container and leave it in the fridge for the night. After that, just use this natural moisturiser every day as your regular moisturiser. If you want to make more of it, just mix the equal amounts of the three ingredients.

    What causes stretch marks?

    Stretch marks have several reasons for appearing:

    • If you are a teenager, you might have stretch marks because of growing too fast. Your skin cannot catch up with your growth spurt, so it stretches too thin, thus causing stretch marks to appear.
    • The same can be said about rapid gain or loss of weight. If your weight slowly increases or decreases, your skin can adapt to the changes pretty easily. However, if you gain or lose kilograms very quickly, the skin loses its elasticity, which is why stretch marks appear.
    • Pregnant people suffer from stretch marks more often than others do. As the body has to accustom to carrying a living thing inside of it, not all of its parts can catch up in time. That is why when the belly grows and the breasts fill up, the skin sometimes cannot handle the pressure.
    • Stretch marks are also a result of having the increased levels of cortisone in the body. Cortisone is a naturally produced hormone that comes from the adrenal glands. If a person has Marfan’s syndrome, Cushing’s syndrome, Ehlers-Danlos syndrome, or any other syndrome that affects the adrenal gland, they might get stretch marks.

    How prevent stretch marks during pregnancy

    There is no proven way that will definitely help you to avoid getting stretch marks during pregnancy. However, here are a few tips that might help to minimise the chances of getting them. Here they are:

    • Eat right. Have a proper diet with balanced meals. Make sure you are getting enough vitamins A and E, as well as omega 3s and antioxidants.
    • Do not forget to exercise while you are pregnant. It will help your skin to keep it elasticity and keep your weight in check. Speaking of…
    • Watch your weight. Eating for two does not mean eating twice as much food as you normally do.
    • Stay hydrated. While it is always important to drink plenty of water, it is absolutely essential during pregnancy.
    • Stay away from harsh chemicals. Look out for sulphates that might be hiding in your bottle of body wash. You can use a cleanser with natural oils as a much healthier alternative.
    • Take some supplements. Vitamin supplements, especially the supplements of vitamins C, E and D can do wonders to your skin’s elasticity.
    • Massage the stretch mark-prone areas with lotions. They should contain vitamins K, C, E and A.

    Are stretch marks bad?

    When it comes down to it, stretch marks are not bad. They do not cause any pain or harm. However, they might itch sometimes, if the skin is too dry. The only thing stretch marks can be bad for is the person’s self-esteem, as some people feel uncomfortable in their own skin because of the way it looks. The only way to deal with that is to embrace the stretch marks as something natural and move on.

    Do stretch marks cause itching?

    Stretch marks might become itchy and even change in colour if the skin is too dry. Usually, it is nothing to worry about. A little bit of lotion should fix that right away. However, if the itch is persistent and does not go away, you should probably ask a doctor for advice.

    Is stretch marks on breast a sign of pregnancy?

    If you notice stretch marks appearing on your breasts, it might be because you are pregnant. However, as we have established before, pregnancy is not the only reason why you might be getting stretch marks. Therefore, you should look out for other symptoms of pregnancy before jumping to conclusions.

    Do stretch marks go away naturally?

    Well, depending on what you mean by ‘go away’. Stretch marks never go away in the sense that the skin returns to the way it used to be. However, they usually become much lighter with time. Some people might notice them fade away very quickly and become barely noticeable, while others might not see any changes for a while (or ever).
