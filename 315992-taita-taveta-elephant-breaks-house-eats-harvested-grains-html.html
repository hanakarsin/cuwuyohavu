Taita Taveta: Elephant breaks into house, eats all harvested grains

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Tuesday, May 28, 2024

A family in Mwatate constituency is in distress after a stray elephant consumed all their harvested grains on the morning of Saturday, September 20.

According to eyewitness, the one-horned animal broke into a store where maize and other cereals had been stored in Kamtonga village and swept it clean.

The home owner was not around as he had left for work.

Elephants on high testosterone can easily to kill humans.

Locals called on Kenya Wildlife Service (KWS) to control the jumbos which were dangerously and fearlessly roaming around in the county causing destruction of crops.

This is not the first time such an incident which has become a norm is reported in Taita Taveta county.

“KWS is not making any efforts to control the jumbos from destroying crops. Do not forget they never compensate farmers on the losses or even the loved ones who die as a result of being killed by the elephants,” lamented Rodrick Mwachanya, a farmer in Kamtonga.

They also accused KWS of failing to respond to their distress calls whenever they have been attacked by a wild animal but only do so when such animals are killed.

“We have lost a number of sheep, goats, and cows as a result of straying leopards and hyenas. And now we live in fear due to the straying jumbos. Our county is slowly becoming a game park even where there are homesteads,” added Mike Mwakio, resident.

Story by Monica Wambugha, TUKO.co.ke correspondent in Taita Taveta county.
