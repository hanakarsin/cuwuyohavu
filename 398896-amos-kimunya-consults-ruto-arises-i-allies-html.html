Amos Kimunya says he consults with Ruto when need arises: &quot;I know him better than his new allies&quot;

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Tuesday, April 9, 2024

- Some 189 lawmakers majorly allied to the Tanga Tanga wing of Jubilee Party had accused the National Assembly majority leader, Amos Kimunya, of causing division in the House

- According to Isiolo woman representative Jehema Jaldesa, Kimunya had lost the trust of many MPs due to alleged biased distribution of Christmas tokens

- The Kipipiri MP, however, said he was not aware Deputy President William Ruto had directed lawmakers to impeach him or vote in a certain way

National Assembly majority leader Amos Kimunya has broken his silence after Deputy President William Ruto's allies launched an onslaught to kick him out of office over alleged dictatorial tendencies.

Speaking to Citizen TV onTuesday, January 5, evening, the Kipipiri Member of Parliament (MP) said he consults with the DP when need arises while insisting he understood the second in command better than his newly found allies in the political scene.

"If the need arises, which has not arisen in the last six months, I consult the DPâ€¦We have consulted before. The DP is my friend. I have known him since 2003. He is not a stranger to me. I know a lot of things about him than most of his new allies know," he said.

Kimunya said he was not aware Ruto had directed lawmakers to impeach him or vote in a certain way in the August House.

"We have a very clear distinction between our work in Parliament and the politics of 2022. I don't care about your 2022 orientation. Whom your support is your personal decision and that will be determined at the ballot. Nobody has ever come and told me the DP has told us to do this because I won't even see why he would want to do it," he maintained.

The MP 's remarks came after Some 189 lawmakers majorly allied to the Tanga Tanga wing of Jubilee accused the majority leader of causing division in the National Assembly.

The parliamentarians vowed to stop at nothing to impeach Kimunya who is less than a year old in the position he took over from Garissa Township MP Aden Duale.

Nominated MP David Sankok took to his Facebook to announce a looming impeachment of a "dictator" once recess was over.

"An impeachment motion against a dictator in the National Assembly leadership is loading," he said.

According to Isiolo Woman Representative Jehema Jaldesa, Kimunya was dividing the House and had lost the trust of many lawmakers due to biased distribution of Christmas tokens.
