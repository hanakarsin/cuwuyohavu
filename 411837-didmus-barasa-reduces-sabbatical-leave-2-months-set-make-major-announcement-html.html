Didmus Barasa Reduces Sabbatical Leave to 2 Months, Set to Make Major Announcement

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Thursday, July 4, 2024

- Vocal Tanga Tanga MP Didmus Barasa took a step back from national politics to focus on the affairs of the Mulembe nation

- He announced a four-month sabbatical leave to allow him to engage and consult his supporters and local political leaders

- The second-time lawmaker said he would soon make his political stand known and address the possibility of Luhya leaders working together

Kimilili lawmaker Didmus Barasa has said he will give the Luhya nation a political direction as far as the 2022 General Election is concerned.

Barasa said he had opted to reduce his sabbatical leave to two months after successfully engaging his constituents and the Mulembe political leadership on the way forward.

''In the last two months, I have been on leave, I reached out to several Luhya leaders. There are a few whom I'm yet to speak to," he said.

Speaking in Kimilili on Wednesday, May 12, Barasa assured his supporters he would soon make his political stand known and address the possibility of Luhya leaders working together.

''I will call for the press again on June 15 to tell the Mulembe nation the route they should take ahead of 2022,'' Barasa stated.

Four-month break

On Sunday, March 21, the legislator announced he would take a four-month sabbatical from national politics and use the recess to consult on his next political step with his supporters.
