Alfred Mutua to Hold Double Birthday with Bongo Singer Rayvanny

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Wednesday, August 21, 2024
  • Mutua announced to his followers that he will be holding a joint birthday party with singer Rayvanny at a Nairobi hotel
  • He assured attendees that they will get into the event for free and get entertained by the Tanzanian musician
  • The birthday party comes weeks after the politician travelled to Tanzania where he met with Rayvanny at his Next Level studios

Machakos governor Alfred Mutua may be in a bad mood following his recent breakup with his wife Lilian but cannot let the hiccup bring sorrow to his life.

Alfred Mutua's birthday

TUKO.co.ke understands Mutua will be turning a year older on Sunday, August 22, and has a special occasion all lined up for him, friends and followers.

The governor will be hosting a double birthday party with his friend, Tanzanian singer Rayvanny at one of Nairobi's top hotels.

According to Mutua, the day will not only be a big one for him and Rayvanny but also a number of people who will also be celebrating their birthdays on that particular day.

In a social media post seen by TUKO.co.ke on Mutua's Facebook page, the birthday party will be held at the Emara Hotel, Ole Sereni.

Guests at the birthday party will eat cake and also get entertained by one of the birthday boys, Rayvanny.

Mutua confirmed to his followers that no one will be charged at the door as it is exclusive but free to enter the event.

"Sunday, August 22nd, is my birthday and also the birthday of Rayvanny - We have decided to hold a special double birthday party for a few people on Sunday afternoon. Please get your free ticket and join us for food and music. See poster below." Mutua wrote.

Kenyans remind him of his breakup

His announcement was received with mixed reaction as a cross-section of Kenyans reminded him about his recent breakup with Lilian.

This comes just days after Mutua confirmed his breakup with his wife.

The politician noted that their split was amicable and they remain friends.

Mutua had earlier visited Tanzania where he met up with Rayvanny at his state of the art studios.

Mutua visited the musician and talked about a number of things including their personal lives and the probability of working together on a number of projects in the near future.

The politician shared photos and videos of himself having some good times at Rayvanny's studio, followed by the caption:

''I had a great time with talented Tanzanian artist Rayvanny (Raymond Shaban Mwakyusa) who was born at the southern tip of Tanzania in Mbeya. Rayvanny - Chui - and I share a birthday (August 22nd) and we spent an afternoon singing and talking of our childhood(s), aspirations and how we can work together to consolidate and grow the talents of East African music.''

He went ahead to disclose that he will soon be launching an initiative to empower talented filmmakers and music artists.
