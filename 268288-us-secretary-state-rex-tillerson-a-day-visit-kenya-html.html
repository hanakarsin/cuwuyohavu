U.S. Secretary of State Rex Tillerson taken ill while on a three day visit in Kenya

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Tuesday, April 23, 2024

- U.S. Secretary of State has been taken ill a day after he landed in the country for crucial bilateral talks with President Uhuru Kenyatta

- A spokesman of the State Department says this is after a couple of long days working on major issues back in the US

- The events which were scheduled to be undertaken by the secretary on Saturday, March 10 have been rescheduled to Sunday, March 11

Events scheduled to be undertaken on the second day of U.S. Secretary of State Rex Tillerson visit to Kenya were on Saturday, March 3 canceled.

According to a spokesman of the State Department, the secretary was not feeling well a day after he landed in the country.

“The secretary is not feeling well after a long couple days working on major issues back home such as North Korea and has canceled his events for the day,” spokesman Steve Goldstein said.

According to a report by Citizen TV, The Secretary of state was set to visit the site of the 1998 bombing of the U.S. Embassy in Nairobi and meet with American diplomats serving at the new U.S. Embassy.

Tillerson was also set to tour Nairobi’s Pumwani Maternity Hospital.

It is reported that his personal doctor is monitoring him and the events he was set to undertake have been rescheduled to Sunday, March 11.

Earlier reporting by TUKO.co.ke indicated that the Secretary of State touched down in Nairobi on Friday, March 9 ahead of crucial bilateral talks with President Uhuru Kenyatta.

Coincidently, his arrival came shortly after president Uhuru and chief Principal of National Super Alliance (NASA) coalition Raila Odinga had held a meeting at Harambee House on Friday, March 9.

He later held a media briefing at the Villa Rosa Kempinski Hotel where he praised the talks between President Kenyatta and NASA leader Raila Odinga, saying the truce was a major milestone in consolidating national unity.
