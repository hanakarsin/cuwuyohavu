5 family members killed in grisly road accident moments after discharging relative from hospital

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Wednesday, May 15, 2024

- The accident happened along the Nairobi-Kangundo road on Tuesday, October 1

- It involved a private car and a bus belonging to the Kenya Bus Service company

- All the occupants of the car died on the spot while several others sustained multiple injuries

Five members of the same family have perished in a grisly accident at Reflector, Kamulu shopping center along the Nairobi-Kangundo road.

The five were in the same car when it collided with a public service vehicle belonging to Kenya Bus Service after the driver tried to over take a canter but collided head on with the bus.

The unfortunate incident was confirmed by Kayole Officer Commanding Police Division (OCPD) Wilson Kosgey who said the five occupants of the car died on the spot.

According to Kosgey, the owner of the vehicle, a Toyota Probox which was heading to Joska from Kamulu, had just been discharged from the hospital and they were heading home.

"The owner of the vehicle had been discharged from the hospital a few minutes ago and they were going home, how I wish he had just remained at the hospital," said the police boss.

Several other victims who had sustained multiple injuries in the accident that occurred on the night of Tuesday, October 1, were rushed to Mama Lucy Hospital

The bodies of the deceased were taken to City mortuary as investigations into the cause of the accident commenced.

According to residents, the area along Kangundo road has been identified as a black spot following the number of lives that have been lost due to accidents.

"From the beginning of the year, I have witnessed about five accidents in this area," noted one of the residents.

While noting that the wreckage of the two vehicles was towed to Kamulu police station, Kosgey called on the relevant authorities to fix the road to avoid any more loss of lives in the area.

"We are appealing to the responsible authorities to come and help us to fix speed bumps and traffic signs to warn those using this road to be careful," he said.
