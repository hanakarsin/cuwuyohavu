Uganda accuses US of trying to overturn Museveni's win

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Saturday, May 4, 2024

- Uganda said a move by the US ambassador to visit Bobi Wine was an indication of trying to undermine the concluded presidential elections

- The envoy, Natalie Brown, had said she wanted to ensure Bobi was safe but was blocked by the military

- Uganda's Spokesperson Ofwono Opwondo urged Natalie to stop meddling in the affairs of the country

Uganda has accused the United States of trying to overturn President Yoweri Museveni's win after its ambassador tried to visit Opposition leader Bobi Wine.

The envoy, Natalie Brown, had said she wanted to ensure Bobi was safe but was blocked from accessing his house located in the outskirts of Kampala by the military.

Uganda's Spokesperson Ofwono Opwondo urged Natalie to stop meddling in the affairs of the country saying she should not do anything outside her diplomatic norms.

"She has been trying to meddle in Uganda's elections to subvert our polls and the will of the people. She should not do anything outside her diplomatic norms," said Ofwono.

He further said Brown had a history of causing trouble in countries she had worked before, adding that the government was watching her.

The National Unity Platform (NUP) party leader has been under house arrest since election day, Thursday, January 14, shortly after he cast his vote.

Election observers

Previously, the US was blocked from taking part in the diplomatic observation of the Uganda polls.

Brown, disclosed the Uganda Electoral Commission (UEC) had denied more than 75% of the election observer accreditations requested by the US.

The US State Department recently reiterated that independent investigations should be carried out into allegations of election irregularities.

Results

The country's electoral commission said Yoweri, 76, beat 10 other presidential candidates to extend his 35-year rule for another term to 2026 after garnering majority vote.

The results which were announced few minutes to 4 pm on Saturday, January 16, showed that Museveni who ran on the National Resistance Movement party ticket garnered 5,851,037 votes to carry the day.

The win translated to 58.64% of the total vote cast, his closest rival Robert Kyagulanyi managed 3,475,298 votes (34.83%) to make him the official opposition leader.
