Anne Waiguru drops threat to sue rapper King Kaka

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Saturday, July 13, 2024

- Governor Waiguru threatened to sue rapper King Kaka for defamation after the musician allegedly linked her to the NYS scandal which happened in 2015

- The rapper mentioned her in his anti-corruption song Wajinga Nyinyi which caused ripples countrywide

- She opted to drop a demand letter claiming the 32-year-old artiste was simply seeking attention

Governor Anne Waiguru shall not proceed with the decision to sue rapper King Kaka for defamation as she had earlier threatened.

The governor said she arrived at the decision after having watched the singer's antics that followed release of his highly publicised seven minute spoken word poetry, Wajinga Nyinyi.

Waiguru, who had no nice words for the 32-year-old, described the father of three as a "confused attention seeking" young man.

"Having watched the antics of King Kaka in the last couple of days with reports of fake assassination attempts and DCI summons, I recognise that heâ€™s a confused attention seeking young man. I have therefore made the decision not to proceed with the King Kaka suit," she said.

The musician had alluded to the financial impropriety at the National Youth Service in 2015 which happened under Waiguru's watch who was by then serving as the Devolution Cabinet Secretary.

He wondered how the politician passed the integrity test to occupy her current office as though she had not been adversely mentioned in relation to the loss of KSh 791 million.

"So Waiguru is still in the office and we forgot NYS scandal? They bought one pen worth KSh 20 at KSh 8,000. It is either we as Kenyans are fools or somebody has taken the marking scheme. The youths are betting with death claiming betting has given them jobs than the government," he said.

In her statement to withdraw the case, she said it was utterly wrong for youth to misuse their God-given talent to attack and slander politicians.

"It is unfortunate when talented youth are taken advantage of by some politicians and used to settle political scores. As I have chosen in previous occasions of this nature, I will contend with their masters," Waiguru added.

A number of renowned lawyers including Martha Karua and Ahmednasir Abdullahi had offered to provide legal assistance to the artiste should the politician make good her threat.

This followed public condemnation on Waiguru by the Kenyan online community.
